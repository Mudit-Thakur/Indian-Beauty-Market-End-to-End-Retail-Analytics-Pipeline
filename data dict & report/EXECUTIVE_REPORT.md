## 🎉 Festival Revenue Impact

| Festival | Transactions | Revenue (₹) | Revenue Share | AOV (₹) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Non-Festival** | 40,087 | 1,30,09,013 | 80.1% | ₹325 |
| **Holi** (Mar) | 3,331 | 10,97,119 | 6.8% | ₹329 |
| **Diwali** (Oct–Nov) | 3,364 | 10,88,336 | 6.7% | ₹324 |
| **Summer** (May–Jun) | 3,218 | 10,51,482 | 6.5% | ₹327 |

> **Insight:** The three festival periods collectively contribute **19.9% of annual revenue** (₹32.4L) across only ~30% of the calendar year. AOV remains stable across festivals vs. non-festival (~₹325–329), indicating festivals drive **volume lift, not ticket size lift**. Marketing investment should focus on transaction frequency, not upsell tactics.

## 👥 RFM Customer Segmentation

| Segment | Customers | % of Base |
|---|---|---|
| **Potential Loyalists** | 327 | 32.7% |
| **At Risk** | 302 | 30.2% |
| **Champions** | 206 | 20.6% |
| **Loyal Customers** | 165 | 16.5% |

> **Key Risk:** 30.2% of the customer base (302 customers) is classified as At Risk — these are customers with declining recency and frequency scores. A targeted win-back campaign (e.g., personalised discount on their top purchased category) for this segment represents the highest-priority CRM action.

> **Opportunity:** Potential Loyalists (327 customers, the largest segment) are one step from becoming Champions. A loyalty nudge programme (e.g., "3 more purchases to unlock VIP status") targeting this cohort would drive the highest incremental LTV.

## 🔄 Cohort Retention

- **Cohort window:** Jan 2024 – Dec 2024 (12 monthly cohorts)
- **Reference retention rate (Month 5, Jan cohort):** 98.2% (`917/934 customers`)
- **Cohort size range:** Largest cohort acquired in Jan 2024 (~934 customers); subsequent cohorts smaller due to uniform distribution

> **Interpretation:** Retention rates are artificially high (>95%) because the synthetic data was generated with uniform random sampling — every customer has an equal probability of transacting in any given month. Real-world cohort retention would show the classic "smile curve" decay (e.g., 100% → 40% → 25% → stabilise). This is documented as a known synthetic data limitation.
